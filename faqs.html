About the Product

The longevity of a perfume's scent varies depending on factors such as the concentration of fragrance oils, individual body chemistry, and the notes used in the perfume. Typically, perfumes can last anywhere from a few hours to several hours or even all day. Companies often provide information about the expected longevity of their perfumes, which can help customers choose the right product for their preferences.



Eau de parfum typically has a higher concentration of fragrance oils, making it more long-lasting compared to eau de toilette or eau de cologne. Eau de toilette has a lighter concentration and is often used for everyday wear, while eau de cologne has an even lighter concentration and is typically associated with refreshing and citrus-based scents.

It is recommended to apply perfume to pulse points, such as the wrists, neck, and behind the ears, as these areas generate heat and can enhance the fragrance's projection. Additionally, some experts suggest spraying the perfume from a slight distance and allowing it to settle on the skin rather than rubbing it in, as this can alter the scent.
